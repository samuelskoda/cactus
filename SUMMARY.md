# My Cactus Collection — Summary

A read-through of the 38 photos (`IMG_9481`–`IMG_9518`). Each photo is one potted
specimen. Identifications come from the handwritten pot tags (originally via PlantNet)
plus a visual check. A few are flagged **tentative** where the tag was illegible or the
plant doesn't obviously match its label.

## By the numbers
- **37** photos / specimens (IMG_9512 was a duplicate of IMG_9511 and was removed)
- **~26** distinct species
- **13** genera
- **3** regions of origin

## By region of origin
- **Mexico & SW USA** — the majority: all Mammillaria, Stenocactus, Astrophytum,
  Coryphantha, Kroenleinia, Ferocactus, and the Disocactus.
- **Southern South America** (Argentina / Brazil / Uruguay) — the Echinopsis/Lobivia,
  Parodia and Gymnocalycium.
- **Andes** (Peru / Bolivia) — Espostoa melanostele and Cumulopuntia boliviana.

## Notes & likely mislabels to double-check
- **"Lobivia ancistrophora" ×3** (IMG_9484, 9502, 9508) — three quite different-looking
  plants share this tag. *Lobivia* is now merged into *Echinopsis*. Worth re-checking; at
  least one may be a different Echinopsis or a Gymnocalycium.
- **IMG_9487 "Echinopsis aurea"** *(still tentative)* — the spiny body reads more like a
  Mammillaria pincushion than a typical Echinopsis globe, but the tag says *aurea* and that
  species can be small and spiny, so it's left flagged.
- **IMG_9491 → Ferocactus sp.** *(tentative)* — tagged *Echinocereus emoryi* (not an accepted
  name); the stout ribbed barrel looks more like a Ferocactus. Owner leans Ferocactus but
  isn't sure, so it stays flagged.
- **IMG_9498 → Parodia × erubescens** — confirmed as the garden **hybrid** (the × marks a
  cross); no longer flagged.
- **IMG_9509 & IMG_9513 "Parodia magnifica"** *(could be leninghausii)* — two separate plants;
  their soft golden spines make them hard to split from *P. leninghausii*.
- **IMG_9500, IMG_9510, IMG_9517, IMG_9518** — tags weren't legible / absent in the
  photos; the owner has since confirmed these (M. compressa, M. vetula, E. oxygona,
  K. grusonii respectively).
- **Kroenleinia grusonii** is the current name for the golden barrel (long known as
  *Echinocactus grusonii*); your tag already uses the new genus.

## The website
`index.html` (with `data.js`) is a self-contained catalogue you can open in a browser.
It shows every plant as a photo card, lets you **filter by genus and by region** and
**search by name**, and opens a detail view per plant. Every plant already has a stable
deep-link with a **stable id independent of the photo filename** (e.g.
`index.html#espostoa-melanostele`), so a future **QR code** on each pot tag keeps working
even after you reshoot and swap photos. To edit any name or description, just change `data.js`.
