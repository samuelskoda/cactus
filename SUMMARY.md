# My Cactus Collection — Summary

A catalogue of the potted cacti (photos `IMG_9481`–`IMG_9518`). Each photo is one
specimen. Identifications are best-effort — from the pot labels plus a visual check — and
several are flagged **tentative** where the plant doesn't clearly match its name.

## By the numbers
- **37** specimens (IMG_9512 was a duplicate of IMG_9511 and was removed)
- **~28** distinct species
- **13** genera
- **3** regions of origin

## By region of origin
- **Mexico & SW USA** — the majority: all Mammillaria, Stenocactus, Astrophytum,
  Coryphantha, Kroenleinia, Ferocactus, and the Disocactus.
- **Southern South America** (Argentina / Brazil / Uruguay) — the Echinopsis/Lobivia,
  Parodia and Gymnocalycium.
- **Andes** (Peru / Bolivia) — Espostoa melanostele, Cumulopuntia boliviana, Matucana aureiflora.

## Notes & open ID questions
- **IMG_9494 → Matucana aureiflora** — reclassified from *Parodia leninghausii*; the label
  reads Matucana aureiflora, a yellow-flowered Peruvian species (unusual for a genus of
  mostly red/orange-flowered plants).
- **IMG_9508 Echinopsis oxygona** — tagged (correctly) *Echinopsis oxygona*; it was long
  grown as *Echinopsis eyriesii*, a small-spined form once treated as a separate species but
  now folded into *E. oxygona*. The *ancistrophora* tag is now only on **IMG_9484 and
  IMG_9502**; of those, 9502 looks consistent while 9484 is nearly spineless and doesn't
  obviously show the hooked ("ancistro-") central spines the name refers to — a mild flag,
  though it may just be a juvenile.
- **IMG_9487 "Echinopsis aurea"** *(tentative)* — the spiny, cylindrical body reads more like
  a Mammillaria pincushion than a typical Echinopsis globe; but *E. aurea* (Lobivia) can be
  small and stiff-spined, so the tag is plausible and it's left flagged rather than changed.
- **IMG_9482 "Coryphantha cornifera"** *(now tentative)* — the many spreading, hooked spines
  don't clearly show *cornifera*'s single stout horn. Alternatives: another Coryphantha, an
  Escobaria, or a hook-spined Mammillaria. Flowers (large, from the growing tip) would help.
- **IMG_9491 → Ferocactus emoryi** *(tentative)* — the pot label actually reads *Ferocactus
  emoryi* (a real Sonoran barrel), not Echinocereus. Plausible, but young barrels are hard
  to pin to species, so it could be misidentified.
- **IMG_9490 (karwinskiana) & IMG_9495 (single "compressa")** *(both tentative)* — the big
  clump IMG_9500 is a confident *M. compressa*, but these two single heads are uncertain and
  may even be the **same** species. To tell *compressa* from *karwinskiana*: flower colour
  (compressa purple-pink; karwinskiana cream with a red midstripe), how they clump (compressa
  offsets densely; karwinskiana splits its head in two), and axil wool/bristles — both bleed
  milky latex.
- **IMG_9498 → Parodia × erubescens** — confirmed as a documented natural **hybrid**,
  *(Osten) D.R.Hunt*, a cross of *P. mammulosa × P. concinna* from **Uruguay** (per Wikipedia).
- **IMG_9509 & IMG_9513 Parodia magnifica** — two separate plants, both confirmed.
- **IMG_9501 Gymnocalycium saglionis** — previously flagged only because the tag was hard to
  read; it matches the clearly-labelled *saglionis* (IMG_9505), so it's now confirmed.
- **Kroenleinia grusonii** is the current name for the golden barrel (long known as
  *Echinocactus grusonii*); the tag already uses the new genus.

## The website
`index.html` (with `data.js`) is a self-contained catalogue you can open in a browser.
It shows every plant as a photo card, lets you **filter by genus and by region** and
**search by name**, and opens a detail view per plant with Wikipedia links for the genus
and species. To edit any name or description, change `data.js`.
